# Track: Montmeló

Barcelona-Catalunya-shaped track (2023 no-chicane layout — the fast T13/T14 final corners, no Mickey-Mouse chicane). Named after the town the real circuit sits in, staying clear of trademarks.

## Completed
- **Machine-traced centerline.** Unlike Champions' Wall (hand-traced control points), the control points were extracted programmatically by `scripts/trace-overlay.py`: it masks the sector-colored racing line (red/cyan/yellow) out of the reference map PNG, keeps connected components ≥ 400 px (the line is split by finish-line marks and DRS dashes; legend swatches are dropped via a bottom-region filter), clusters pixels into 7-px grid cells, orders the centroids by nearest-neighbor walk, applies a window-5 moving average, and emits an ordered loop.
- The traced loop (1692 px points) is post-processed by `scripts/trace-to-controls.py` (exact invocation documented in the `montmelo.mjs` header): oriented clockwise (real driving direction), rotated so index 0 sits at the checkered finish mark (image px 1377, 732), then **Douglas-Peucker simplified (eps 2 px)** — straights collapse to collinear endpoints (no pixel-noise wobble), corners keep their traced density — and long segments subdivided (≤120 px) so interior straight points stay collinear under the spline. 113 control points. Image is 1920×1035 at **scale 2.2** centered on origin: world = ((px − 960) × 2.2, (py − 517.5) × 2.2).
- **Scale history:** first cut used scale 4 with 120 evenly spaced control points → 24k world-unit lap, >1:00 lap times, endless straights, wobbly main straight (pixel noise survived even resampling). Playtest feedback drove the rescale to 2.2 (13.1k lap, between Temple of Speed 11.4k and Champions' Wall 12.6k) and the DP-based simplification.
- **Spline-curvature relaxation:** DP preserves true corner tightness, so at scale 2.2 the tightest corners dipped to spline radius 40 (< half-width 65 → self-intersecting offset polygons). Control points get Laplacian-relaxed against the *sampled spline* (not the control polygon — uneven spacing makes spline curvature overshoot the control-polygon estimate) until every spline triple's circumradius ≥ 78. Converged in 21 iterations; final spline min radius 77.3.
- `scripts/tracks/montmelo.mjs` runs the control points through `catmullRomLoop(ctrl, 3)` → 339 centerline points. Zero self-intersections.
- Width 130 (like Temple of Speed), gravel outside runoff (nominal 90), grass inside (nominal 50), 12 checkpoints, startIndex 0 on the main straight heading west.
- **Per-point clamped runoff widths** via `clampedRunoffWidths()` in `_shared.mjs`. At scale 2.2 the esses fold track sections to within ~180 world units (free gap ~50 after asphalt), so uniform 90-unit gravel made adjacent sections' runoff polygons overlap — walls crossed visually, and cars could drive through them because the collision probe's nearest-centerline-index flips inside the overlap zone. The helper clamps each point's runoff to: (1) half the free gap to the nearest other section (cyclic separation > 15 points), (2) local curvature radius − half-width on the concave side of corners, then (3) a fixpoint pass that builds the actual offset polygon and shrinks widths at any remaining crossing segments until both wall loops have zero self-intersections. Floor 2 — at the tightest corners the wall hugs the asphalt.
- Two real-world DRS zones: back straight after T9 and main straight before the final corner. Anchor indices computed in the track module via `idxAtPx()` (nearest centerline point to a reference-image pixel), so they survive rescales/resamples automatically.
- Reference overlay `public/inspect-overlays/montmelo.png` (gitignored; Wikimedia Commons "2023 F1 CourseLayout Spain.svg" rendered at 1920 px), `referenceOverlay` scale = track SCALE constant, alpha 0.4.
- Registered in `MenuScene.TRACKS` + `TrackKey` union. Verified in inspector: asphalt centerline sits on the map's colored line around the whole lap (esses, T10–T12 stadium loop, final corners, finish at the checkered mark). Race smoke-tested: loads, AI drives off cleanly.

## Architecture Decisions
- **Machine tracing over hand tracing.** When the reference map draws the racing line in distinct flat colors, color-masking + nearest-neighbor walk gives a denser, more faithful centerline in minutes than hand-reading pixel coordinates. `scripts/trace-overlay.py` is reusable for any map with a flat-colored line; hand-tracing stays the fallback for noisy/aerial references.
- **DP simplification over even resampling.** Even arc-length resampling spreads pixel noise into every control point (subtly wobbly straights) and rounds off tight corners (spacing-limited). Douglas-Peucker does the opposite: straights become exactly straight, corners keep full traced fidelity — then a separate spline-curvature relaxation pass handles the offset-polygon constraint explicitly instead of relying on resampling to smooth it away by accident.
